Company Overview

Wood Mackenzie is a leading provider of data-driven insights, analysis, and consulting services across energy, chemicals, metals, and mining sectors. Their clientele includes energy producers, governments, financial institutions, and corporations, all seeking intelligence to navigate the complex, evolving landscape of natural resources.

The company offers platforms and tools that deliver robust analytics on power, hydrogen, carbon, supply chains, and more. Their solutions blend real-time data, proprietary analytics, strategic consultancy, and tailored insights to empower decision-makers across industries.

My Impact

During my time working in energy analytics, I developed a strong foundation in power markets and regional ISOs while contributing to projects that blended data science with real-world impact. I explored supervised, unsupervised, and computer vision-based anomaly detection methods, ultimately designing a proof of concept that I presented to stakeholders as a pathway toward workflow optimization.

Alongside this, I collaborated on building and monitoring large-scale machine learning models to boost product performance and deliver predictive insights. To expand data coverage, I designed SQL queries that successfully linked anonymized ISO bid data with known generators, overcoming limitations in source information. I also analyzed backend systems to resolve customer-reported issues—strengthening data integrity and client trust in the platform.

Co-ops were given active roles and frequently recruited for cross-team projects, allowing me to engage in multiple facets of Wood Mackenzie’s products and services. This kept me constantly challenged while gaining knowledge beyond my core responsibilities.
